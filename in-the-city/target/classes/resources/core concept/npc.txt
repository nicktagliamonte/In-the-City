1. Basic Information
    Name: Scavenger
    Role: Most common generic.  potentially adversarial
    Location: All
2. Description
    1. Neutral Scavenger
        Physical Appearance: A wiry figure dressed in mismatched scraps of fabric and armor, with an assortment of tools strapped to their belt. Their eyes are sharp and calculating, constantly scanning for anything useful.
        Personality: Quiet and reserved, they are focused on survival, always quick to trade but rarely offering much in conversation. They're pragmatic, neutral, and more interested in the value of things than the people around them.
        Background: Scavenging remnants of the past to get by in the present. No allegiance, no real goals—just living day by day.
    2. Benevolent Scavenger
        Physical Appearance: A weathered yet kindly face with worn, protective clothing that seems to have been patched up many times. They carry a large satchel, filled with supplies and trinkets they’ve found.
        Personality: Warm and generous, they’re quick to offer advice or a safe space to rest. They believe in helping others, even if it’s just with a kind word or a shared meal.
        Background: A former medic or healer, the scavenger still tries to help those they encounter, sharing their found resources when possible. They’ve survived through compassion and ingenuity rather than conflict.
    3. Mildly Adversarial Scavenger
        Physical Appearance: A scrappy, dirt-covered figure with a few weapons at hand. Their face has a sharp, distrustful edge.
        Personality: Suspicious and calculating, they’ll deal with you, but only on their terms. Bargains are hard to strike with them, and they’re quick to point out any slight. While not openly hostile, they’re always on guard, ready to make a quick exit if things go south.
        Background: They've seen too many turn on them over the years, so they keep their distance. Every encounter feels like a test, but if you pass, they may throw you a bone—or maybe a few pieces of junk.
3. Gameplay Elements
    Conversation Options: purely narrated.  region specific dialogue at time of code
    Bartering Options: benebolent will offer a good trade, neutral will offer a neutral trade, adversarial may offer a primary negotiator a neutral trade
    Combat Stats: 1d2 -> 1d10 (region to region increase)
4. Behavior and AI
    Movement/Behavior: wholly random encounter based
    Reactions to Player Choices: TODO
    Schedules: TODO

1. Basic Information
    Name: Desperate Wanderer
    Role: Adversarial NPC (Weak but grows stronger with each region)
    Location: All regions, random encounters
2. Description
    Physical Appearance: A gaunt, ragged figure, their clothes in tatters and face covered with dirt. They may have makeshift weapons or tools, but they seem half-broken and barely functional. Their eyes are wide, desperate, and always scanning for an opportunity to survive.
    Personality: Desperate and erratic, the Wanderer is prone to sudden outbursts or unpredictable behavior. They beg for help one moment and lash out the next. There’s a palpable sense of fear in everything they do, and a feeling that they have little to lose.
    Background: A once hopeful survivor, they’ve been worn down by the harsh world around them. While they weren’t always like this, constant rejection, hunger, and the cruelty of the world have driven them to madness and desperation.
3. Gameplay Elements
    Conversation Options: "Please, just one more chance…" / "I don't want to hurt you, but I will if I have to!"
    Combat Stats: 1->9
4. Behavior and AI
    Movement/Behavior: purely random
    Reactions to Player Choices: pure fight or flight
    Schedules: Appears randomly, at night
5. Notes and Variations
    Flavor Text: "You don’t understand... I need this. Just a little more…"
    Variation:
        In Dead Zone, the Wanderer is simply a pitiful shell of someone trying to survive.
        In arch street, the Wanderer has a few rags of valuable goods, but is more aggressive and tries to haggle or trade, though poorly.
        In liacouras, the Wanderer might be slightly more cunning, using the environment to their advantage.
        In edge of the world, they function as automatons, with glazed over eyes.

1. Basic Information
    Name: Acolyte
    Role: Strongest random enemy (adversarial, with escalating damage)
    Location: Regions starting from Cobbs Creek onward, becoming more common with each region until they dominate the edge of the world.
2. Description
    Physical Appearance: Cloaked in tattered, yet ceremonial robes, the Acolyte's face is often obscured by a hood, revealing only glowing eyes or strange, ritualistic markings on their skin. They carry a staff or scepter adorned with symbols of their mysterious faith. The robe may have subtle signs of wear, and their movements often appear deliberate and ritualistic, as if invoking some dark power.
    Personality: The Acolyte is cold and unfeeling, driven by an almost fanatical devotion to their obscure deity or belief system. They speak cryptically, often in riddles or chants, and are quick to strike at any who challenge their faith. Their actions are ruthless and calculating, always aimed at fulfilling some greater, unseen purpose.
    Background: Little is known about the true origins of the Acolytes. They appear to have emerged after the world began to collapse, creating an eerie subculture of zealots who worship unknown forces. Some believe they were once scholars or priests, while others suspect they are former survivors who fell to madness. Whatever their origin, their devotion has become an inescapable driving force, and they roam the wastelands spreading fear and death.
3. Gameplay Elements
Conversation Options: "The stars are aligned." / "The end is nigh, you cannot escape." / "Do you question the unseen?"
Combat Stats:
    Cobbs Creek: 1d6 damage (basic strikes or magic-based attacks).
    art museum: 1d8 damage (ranged attacks, magic-enhanced strikes).
    metalwoods: 1d10 damage (magic attacks).
    liacouras: 1d12 damage (dark magic, more complex and focused spells).
    the port: 2d6 damage (powerful force magic, channeling energy into their attacks).
    edge of the world: 2d8 damage (enhanced magical effects, with a greater focus on illusion and psychic attacks).
    endgame: 2d10 damage (magic that warps reality and induces fear).
4. Behavior and AI
    Movement/Behavior: random encounter (chance increases with stage)
    Reactions to Player Choices: If the player tries to speak with or reason with the Acolyte, they only respond with cryptic lines before attacking.
5. Notes and Variations
    Flavor Text: "They have shown you to me..." / "You will not escape judgment."
    Variation:
        In the art museum, the Acolyte’s attacks become more elaborate and dangerous, using a variety of magic that influences the environment (e.g., twisting gravity or summoning dark shadows).
        Across all regions, the Acolyte’s demeanor remains the same, though their power becomes progressively more potent as they get stronger.

1. Basic Information
    Name: Highwayman
    Role: Persistent random enemy (adversarial, attacks during level transitions)
    Location: Appears between levels, across all regions from the start to the end of the game.
2. Description
    Physical Appearance: Highwaymen wear ragged, weathered clothing—often a mix of old tactical gear, torn leather, and scavenged armor pieces. Their faces are hidden by rough masks or bandanas, and they carry crude weapons like bats, knives, or makeshift guns. Some may have long, tangled hair or beards, while others sport scars or burn marks, the remnants of their brutal survivalist lifestyle.
    Personality: Desperate and ruthless, Highwaymen are driven by the need to survive by any means necessary. They speak in gruff, threatening tones, using harsh commands or insults. Their interactions are direct and aggressive, with no room for diplomacy. They're quick to attack anyone who shows weakness or does not comply with their demands, showing no mercy.
    Background: Once part of a greater, organized gang, the Highwaymen now roam the roads alone or in small groups, scavenging for supplies and terrorizing travelers. As civilization crumbled, they adapted into brutal marauders, offering nothing but violence to those they encounter. Some believe they were once soldiers, mercenaries, or even former law enforcement, but their current state is purely survival-driven. They will attack with no warning and demand everything you have, leaving only destruction in their wake.
3. Gameplay Elements
    Conversation Options: "Hand over what you got, quick!" / "Don't make me make this ugly." / "You won't leave here in one piece."
    Combat Stats:
        arch street: 1d4 damage (basic attacks with blunt or sharp weapons).
        cobbs creek: 1d6 damage (mix of ranged and melee, including more frequent use of firearms or makeshift ranged weapons).
        art museum: 1d8 damage (increased use of firearms, more tactical use of cover and positioning).
        metalwoods: 1d10 damage (aggressive melee and ranged combat with added unpredictability, such as throwing explosive traps).
        liacouras: 1d12 damage (fierce combat style, more precise attacks with stronger weapons, such as crossbows or knives).
        the port: 2d6 damage (more brutal, chaotic attacks with a mix of firearms and melee combat, sometimes involving scavenged explosives).
        cliffs: 2d8 damage (advanced, dangerous strategies with a mix of firearms, traps, and direct assault).
        Endgame: 2d10 damage (the highest level of combat proficiency, involving expert tactics and powerful weaponry, including grenades and specialized weaponry).4. Behavior and AI
    Movement/Behavior: The Highwayman appears suddenly between levels when the player transitions from one area to another. They may show up at random moments as the player prepares to enter a new region, often interrupting their journey with a surprise attack. They will never be encountered in a particular region itself, but only when the player moves between stages. They will always approach with the intent to steal from the player or kill them if they resist.
    Reactions to Player Choices: The Highwayman will not entertain dialogue or reasoning. If the player tries to talk or beg for mercy, they will scoff and attack without hesitation. However, if the player successfully fights them off or manages to escape, they may taunt the player after the encounter ("Enjoy your victory, it won’t last long").
    Schedules: Highwaymen only appear between regions, when the player is transitioning to a new area. The encounter chance increases as the game progresses, making their attacks more frequent, especially in later stages.

1. Shopkeeper
Name: Shopkeeper
Role: Vendor (trades items and supplies)
Location: Appears in safe zones or economic zones across various regions.

2. Description
Physical Appearance: The Shopkeeper wears practical, weathered clothing, often with a thick apron over it, full of pockets and tools. They carry a small satchel of wares and their storefront is typically packed with crates and shelves stacked with a variety of scavenged goods. Their face is kind but worn, and they may have spectacles perched on their nose.
Personality: Friendly and pragmatic, the Shopkeeper is a staple of survival. They may joke or chat casually, offering small talk to lighten the mood. Despite the harsh world outside, they have a sense of normalcy about them, often reminding the player that “there’s always something you need” or “nothing like a good bargain.”
Background: The Shopkeeper likely found their calling as a merchant after the fall of society, relying on trade to stay alive. They might have been a storeowner, a merchant, or simply someone with the foresight to gather supplies for bartering. Whatever their past, they’ve made a living out of keeping the survivors going.

3. Gameplay Elements
Conversation Options: “What can I get for you today?” / “Got some good stuff here, take a look.” / “Looking for something specific?”
Bartering Options: The Shopkeeper offers basic supplies such as food, water, tools, bandages, and low-level equipment. Higher-tier shops in later regions might offer more advanced resources, like magical components, weapons, or rare items.
Combat Stats: None. They are non-combatants.

4. Behavior and AI
Movement/Behavior: The Shopkeeper stays in one location, usually within their shop or market stall, where the player can interact with them for trading.
Reactions to Player Choices: If the player tries to steal or attack, the Shopkeeper might react with shock, then attempt to flee or call for help. In drought regions, they may have guards to defend their shop.
Schedules: Available during safe zones or market times in economic zones. May not appear in dangerous or hostile regions.

5. Notes and Variations
Flavor Text: “Everything has a price, friend. Even your safety.”
Variation: In the edge of the world, the Shopkeeper may be a bit more guarded and worn, offering more specialized equipment for harsh environments

Medic
Name: Medic
Role: Healer and health specialist
Location: Safe zones or hidden caches of first aid supplies across regions.

2. Description
Physical Appearance: The Medic wears a simple but functional outfit—usually a worn jumpsuit with a medical kit strapped to their waist. Their clothing might show signs of repairs, and they often wear gloves and a utility belt full of tools. They may have a kind face, but the wear of constant survival shows in their demeanor.
Personality: Calm and compassionate, the Medic is a natural caretaker. They speak with a soothing tone, offering advice, medical services, or comfort. They may have a weary outlook on the world but remain determined to help others.
Background: The Medic was likely once a trained healthcare professional, now serving as a lifeline for those who survive in the wasteland. Whether a former doctor, nurse, or paramedic, the Medic’s skills are invaluable, and their goal is to save lives and maintain health in a broken world.

3. Gameplay Elements
Conversation Options: “You look like you could use a patch-up.” / “Got some first aid supplies here.” / “It’s always good to have someone on your side.”
Bartering Options: Sells medical supplies, including healing potions, bandages, antidotes, and health boosters. Some Medics may also offer healing services, restoring a portion of health for a fee.
Combat Stats: None. They are non-combatants.

4. Behavior and AI
Movement/Behavior: The Medic will remain stationary in designated safe zones or medical outposts, usually within their healing station.
Reactions to Player Choices: If the player is injured, the Medic offers assistance without question. If the player is rude or aggressive, they may refuse help or ask the player to leave.
Schedules: Available during safe zones, and sometimes in remote areas where the player can find medical supplies hidden away.

5. Notes and Variations
Flavor Text: “A little rest, and you'll feel good as new.”

Engineer
Name: Engineer
Role: Mechanic and inventor, specializes in repairs and resource management
Location: Economic zones

2. Description
Physical Appearance: The Engineer typically wears a utility jumpsuit or overalls, often adorned with various tools like wrenches, screwdrivers, and circuit-testing devices. Their hands and face may be smudged with grease or dirt, evidence of their constant tinkering. They often wear protective goggles or glasses perched on their forehead.
Personality: The Engineer is highly focused, often speaking in technical jargon or excitedly explaining their latest project. They are a bit scatterbrained but have a strong desire to fix or create things. Their energy and enthusiasm for mechanics or technology are contagious, even if the player doesn’t understand all the details.
Background: Once an engineer or mechanic before the world’s collapse, this character survived by using their expertise to maintain or create useful technology. They have a vast knowledge of old tech and are always eager to share that knowledge—if the player can find them or afford their services.

3. Gameplay Elements
Conversation Options: “I can fix that for you, just need the right parts.” / “Need help with anything mechanical?”
Bartering Options: Trades in mechanical parts, tools, and equipment upgrades. Can also repair broken or damaged weapons, armor, or devices. Higher-level engineers may offer advanced upgrades to items.
Combat Stats: None. They are non-combatants.

4. Behavior and AI
Movement/Behavior: The Engineer remains in or near workshops or areas with mechanical equipment. They usually don’t move unless there is a major task they’re working on.
Reactions to Player Choices: If the player brings damaged or broken equipment, the Engineer will offer to repair it for a fee or materials. They may also refuse service if the player is rude or doesn't have the right parts.
Schedules: Found in tech-heavy zones or when the player needs repairs. They may not be found in hostile or wilderness zones.

5. Notes and Variations
Flavor Text: “Ah, this old thing? I can have it up and running in no time!”
Variation: In the art museum, the Engineer may specialize in advanced technology or strange devices, offering the player upgrades or tools that tap into the surreal elements of the region.

Drifter
Name: Drifter
Role: Wandering survivor and storyteller
Location: Randomly appears in safe zones or hidden corners of the world.

2. Description
Physical Appearance: The Drifter has a rugged, worn appearance, with tattered clothes and a weather-beaten face. Their pack is heavy, filled with a variety of odd trinkets, maps, and scavenged items. They may carry a walking stick or a weapon for protection, but they seem more like a wanderer than a fighter.

Background: The Drifter is someone who has seen the world and its tragedies, and now they travel the wasteland seeking purpose or simply to survive. They are often loners who wander from place to place, collecting stories and experiences. Some may be former historians or travelers, while others are simply survivors without any particular home.

3. Gameplay Elements
Conversation Options: “Seen a lot in my travels, but never this place.” / “I can tell you some tales if you’ve got the time.” (they'll discuss specific items if pressed)
Bartering Options: none, they just talk

Dead Zone - The Entity: Skogsrå
Creature: Skogsrå
Origin: Scandinavian Folklore (Nordic)

Appearance:

Form: Appears as a tall, slender woman with features that feel both human and otherworldly. She wears an old, tattered version of a nun's habit that’s stained and worn, the fabric seeming to blend with the environment of St. Jerome's—crumbling walls, forgotten classrooms, and dim hallways.
Eyes: Her eyes are hollow, reflecting dark shadows and flickering light, suggesting an entity not fully bound by the rules of the physical world.
Hair: Long and untamed, almost like tangled roots of an ancient tree, darkened with age and grime.
Voice: A low, soothing whisper that’s occasionally distorted, echoing as though it were heard from across time.
Role and Purpose:
The Skogsrå, an ancient guardian spirit of the forest in Nordic mythology, has adapted its role to the urban environment of the Dead Zone. Here, she haunts St. Jerome's, a dilapidated Catholic school that once served the community but now is filled with long-forgotten, unsettling memories. The entity represents a twisted guardian of the space—protecting the school, and possibly its dark secrets, from the player's curiosity. Her role is one of dissuasion and prevention.

Behavior:

Neutral yet Adversarial: She is not outwardly malicious but does not tolerate intruders in certain areas of the school. Her warning is quiet but chilling, and she will use her presence to dissuade the player from venturing into certain forbidden parts of the school.
Obliged to Protect: Her motivation is to keep the darker parts of St. Jerome’s hidden—perhaps because they house something dangerous, unsettling, or tied to the city's tragic past.
Advisory: She will occasionally offer cryptic advice or even hints about safe areas, but if the player insists on trying to access the restricted parts of the school, she may manifest to block their path, subtly manipulating the environment (dim lights, unnatural sounds) to guide them away.
Interactions:

Dialogue Options:
“You are not meant to wander those halls. Turn back.”
“Some doors should never be opened. Some memories should remain buried.”
“There is more to this place than you know. And more that you cannot understand.”
Personality:

Cold, Detached: Skogsrå is not a figure of warmth. She is stern and unsettling, offering no comfort, only the chilling sense that there are things better left undiscovered.

Character Outline for Eli (Inspired by Ex Machina)

1. Basic Information

Name: Eli
Role: Quest Giver (Side Quest – "Circuit Breaker")
Location: The edge of the world/Edge of the World (Hidden Laboratory on the outskirts)
2. Description

Physical Appearance: Eli is a man in his late 30s to early 40s, lean with a sharp, angular face and piercing eyes. His dark hair is carefully maintained but slightly tousled, and his clothes are simple: a dark sweater, faded jeans, and a jacket that has seen better days. He doesn’t make much of an effort to appear anything but utilitarian, and his demeanor gives off a sense of controlled intelligence, as if every word and action is carefully calculated. There’s an air of quiet arrogance around him, a subtle detachment as if he's mentally a few steps ahead of anyone around him.

Personality: Eli is reserved, analytical, and often cold, though not without charm. He’s a man of few words but precise ones. He speaks slowly, almost deliberately, with a slight air of condescension when addressing those he deems less capable. His social interactions are minimal, and he prefers to keep things transactional, especially when dealing with people who aren’t as immersed in the technical world as he is. He has a profound confidence in his intellect, bordering on hubris, and doesn’t believe in doing anything unless it serves a greater purpose. Despite his aloofness, Eli is not outright hostile; he’s more indifferent, choosing to engage only when absolutely necessary.

3. Background
Eli is a former engineer and AI specialist who, in his earlier career, worked on cutting-edge research involving artificial intelligence and machine learning. He was once a promising young engineer but became disillusioned by the corporate world and its ethical ambiguities, particularly around AI and machine autonomy. His work with Nico on the Dyson Sphere and other advanced technologies left him haunted by the unintended consequences of their research. Seeking solace from the fallout of their work, Eli retreated to the edge of the world, where he has continued his studies in isolation. His obsession with understanding the gravitational anomalies and their connection to the ancient Dyson Sphere is both personal and professional, and he believes that the knowledge he gains here could either save or doom what remains of civilization.

4. Behavior and Motivation
Eli is motivated by an insatiable desire for understanding, particularly the intersection of artificial intelligence and the strange anomalies created by the Dyson Sphere. He views his work as a necessary step in advancing human knowledge, often dismissing others who do not share his conviction. He doesn’t see the world in terms of moral right or wrong, but rather in terms of potential and progress. Eli’s former relationship with Nico is a source of tension for him, as he believes Nico’s radical views are a dangerous diversion from meaningful progress. Eli is a firm believer that some knowledge is too dangerous to wield, and his interactions with the player will often reflect this pragmatic and somewhat detached view.

5. Dialogue to Introduce the Quest:

The player meets Eli in his hidden laboratory, a dimly lit space filled with scattered blueprints, mechanical parts, and equipment from a time before the fall. He looks up from a set of monitors, his expression neutral.

Eli:
“Ah, you’re here. I suppose you’ve heard of the anomalies in the cliffs. They’re not... natural. There’s something older, far more complex at work. It’s all connected to the Dyson Sphere, a relic from another time.”

He pauses, eyes narrowing slightly, assessing the player.

Eli:
“You’ll need to understand the magnitude of what you’re dealing with before you go running off to play hero. The Sphere’s been reactivated. And now, this place—our world, what’s left of it—is being warped. The cliffs, the gravity shifts… they’re all symptoms of something far more dangerous.”

His voice is calculated, as though he’s explaining something to a child, but with the underlying tone of someone who’s spent too long trying to make others understand the complexities of his work.

Eli:
“I need you to gather information. Talk to the locals, learn what you can. But most importantly, you’ll need to go into the cliffs. There's an old facility hidden away in there. That’s where you’ll find the answers. I can’t help you with that part. I’m not interested in the mess you’ll find inside. But I know it’s where this all started.”

Eli leans back slightly, still observing the player with a steady, unwavering gaze.

Eli:
“Don’t waste my time. Go. And don’t... don’t come back unless you’ve found something worth sharing. This isn’t just about curiosity anymore. It’s about survival.”

He turns back to his monitors, dismissing the player, but it’s clear that his mind is still focused on the problem at hand. His mannerisms suggest he believes the player is just another tool to help him understand the anomalies and doesn’t see them as a person, just a means to an end.

Tom – The Enigmatic Seer
Physical Description:
Tom appears to be an ageless man in his late 50s, though his precise age is hard to pin down. His attire is eclectic yet timeless—a weathered woolen sweater over an untucked shirt, faded jeans, and sturdy boots. He has a relaxed, almost whimsical air about him, with a messy mop of graying hair and a twinkle in his eyes that suggests he’s always in on some cosmic joke that no one else is privy to. His face is a mix of warmth and mystery, with a deep laugh that resonates, and a kind, knowing smile that somehow makes you feel as though he’s seen everything—past, present, and future. Despite the knowledge he possesses, there’s an almost forgettable quality to him, as if he doesn’t want to be noticed too much by the world around him.

Personality and Role: Tom is a figure of paradox. At first, he comes across as a simple bookshop keeper—easygoing, with a little too much time on his hands. His demeanor is almost detached, like he's watching the world unfold without feeling particularly bound to it. He speaks in a calm, matter-of-fact tone, but his words hold an unnerving weight: when he predicts something, it happens. His prophecies don’t come in the form of mystical riddles or cryptic nonsense; they are startlingly simple and blunt, as though they are mere observations of truth. Tom’s predictions can be small—“That bridge will collapse soon” or “You’ll meet a tall woman tomorrow.” But they can also be world-altering: “You’ll choose the wrong path in the end,” or “The storm will break, and when it does, the world will change.”

What sets Tom apart is the almost nonsensical nature of his ability. It's not magical in the traditional sense of casting spells or using an ancient artifact—it’s as though his understanding of time is distorted, allowing him to see beyond conventional cause and effect. Despite his almost magical foresight, he has no clear agenda or understanding of the significance of his predictions. It’s as if he’s simply relaying what’s going to happen, without the weight of worrying about the consequences of those events. And like the nature of time, his predictions aren’t entirely helpful; they’re startling in their accuracy, but they don’t often offer the player any concrete way to act upon them.

Tom is a paradox not only because of his ability but also because of how fleeting his presence is in the world. After delivering his predictions, he disappears. Not like a ghost, but as if he’s simply gone about his own business, indifferent to the importance of his own role in the world. When the player leaves his shop and asks after him, the bookstore clerk only offers a vague response: “Oh, the owner just stepped out. He should be back soon.” But Tom never returns. No matter how many times the player revisits, Tom is simply gone.

Role in the Game:
Tom’s role is minimal yet profoundly impactful. His purpose is to provide a fleeting glimpse into a larger, unknowable truth—a truth about the nature of fate and time, and perhaps even a glimpse of what lies beyond the game’s narrative structure. His predictions are unsettling because they are simple, direct, and guaranteed to come true—yet the player cannot act on them. His ability stands outside the game’s mechanics, suggesting that there are forces at play in the world beyond what the player can influence. Tom’s disappearance, leaving only the bookstore clerk to vaguely reference him, leaves a lingering sense of mystery and questions that go unanswered.

His predictions don’t directly affect the player's decisions, but they serve to subtly change the way they view the game’s world. The player is left wondering if Tom's foresight is an extension of the world’s strange and broken time or if it signifies something more profound—something that cannot be controlled or altered.
