the broken chain
Quest Overview
While searching for basic supplies in the Dead Zone, the player stumbles upon a partially hidden path leading to an overgrown cul-de-sac. At the end of the road, they find a locked house with a handwritten note on the door. The note is smudged, but it reads:

“Left in a hurry. If you find the bike and fix it, you can get what's inside. My gift, for anyone who still cares to survive.”

Nearby, leaning against a fence, is a rusty bicycle with a broken chain. Fixing it appears simple but will require specific resources, all scattered around the cul-de-sac. The house itself is locked, and the note implies the bike is somehow key to unlocking it.

Quest Steps
Examine the Bike

The player inspects the bicycle and finds the chain is broken, with the bike rendered unusable. They notice that it could be repaired with a few pieces of scrap and a basic tool kit.
Gather Resources

Nearby areas in the cul-de-sac contain some scrap metal, abandoned tools, and potentially usable items. As the player explores, they find pieces they need to repair the chain, though a few items are buried or hidden under debris.
A total of 2 pieces of scrap and a wrench are needed to repair the bike.
Repair the Bike

Once the resources are gathered, the player can attempt to fix the bike. Upon repairing it, they find a mechanism attached to the chain that, when rotated, triggers a click from the lock on the house's door. The house is now accessible.
Enter the House

Inside, the player finds a small cache of useful items—a medkit, some rations, and a water container. There's also a journal that briefly recounts the life of the former resident, describing their struggles to survive in the aftermath of the city's collapse and their attempts to make this house a sanctuary.
Moral Choice (Optional)

The journal entry ends with a plea: “Please, if you have strength, leave something for those who come after.” The player can choose to leave one item from their inventory as a small gesture for future survivors, which grants a slight boost in their ethical alignment.
Alternatively, they can ignore the plea and take all items, with no negative consequence beyond missing the alignment boost.
