Optional Side Quest: Fleeting Memories
Objective: Help a lost soul find closure by unraveling the truth of their memories—but uncovering the truth may mean confronting darker realities.

Quest Overview
In the Liacouras Haunts, the player encounters a spectral figure, Elias, who appears lost and fragmented. Elias speaks of someone they loved deeply, but their memories are fractured and hazy, and they don’t seem entirely sure of who they are or what happened to them. As the player agrees to help Elias recover these lost memories, they are drawn into a disorienting journey, where every step seems to bring new contradictions, eroded realities, and subtle hints that Elias might not be entirely trustworthy.

The quest takes the player through a series of memory fragments, each one pulling them deeper into Elias’s life while twisting their understanding of reality. At first, Elias’s memories appear tragic and heartfelt, but as they collect more pieces, the memories grow increasingly ominous, revealing a truth that suggests Elias is not the sympathetic figure they first seemed to be.

Quest Steps
The Illusive Search

Quest Begins: Elias asks the player to help them find “something precious” they’ve lost—fragments of a memory involving someone they loved. They lead the player to locations scattered around Liacouras Walk, places that hold fragments of their story.

It’s her—his loved one, beaming as she catches him by surprise. Her friends follow a few steps behind, sharing a quiet look before they stop, choosing to keep a slight distance. There’s a flicker of hesitation among them as she joins him on the bench.
                As she sits beside him, the memory holds a distinct warmth but also a nervous undertone. They laugh easily at first, talking about small things. The loved one seems cheerful, her eyes lighting up as she mentions her plans for the upcoming weekend. But Elias’s smile falters as he interrupts her. His words are friendly, almost affectionate, but there’s a sharpness in his tone.
                “We barely ever see each other now, and we go to the same school,” he says, an edge creeping into his voice. “What’s it gonna be like in two weeks when we can’t just rely on running into one another on campus?”
                She shifts uncomfortably, glancing toward her friends, who are still lingering just within view but keep their distance. Her smile fades, replaced by a look of mild unease, but she responds gently, brushing it off with a small laugh. She tells him they’ll figure it out, her voice light but strained, as though trying to reassure him without committing.
                The conversation continues, her responses growing more careful. Though she keeps her tone light, her gaze often strays to her friends, as if looking for an exit. Elias, either not noticing or deliberately ignoring her discomfort, presses on, mentioning the end of the semester and his disappointment about her spending less time with him. His words come off as subtle accusations, slipping in under the guise of humor.
                As she stands to leave, she gives him a parting smile that doesn’t reach her eyes. She rejoins her friends, and they exchange worried glances before walking away with her, casting a final look back at him. The memory lingers just a moment longer, capturing Elias sitting alone, watching as she grows smaller in the distance.

Real version: The memory begins in a quiet corner of an older version of the tech center, around 2016. The lighting is harsh, fluorescent, casting everything in a sterile, cold glow. The Final Revelation
Resolution: In the final scene, Elias’s true nature is revealed: they were a possessive, dangerous figure who drove their loved one away—or worse. As Elias’s full story emerges, the environment around the player begins to warp, the grid of streets around willington becoming a surreal, shifting landscape of distorted memories and eerie whispers.

Outcome: The quest ends with the player leaving the courtyard, unsettled and with lingering doubts. Elias’s final words echo in their mind, hinting that the quest was not about closure but about entrapping another soul in their twisted reality. The player gains no tangible reward, only the unsettling awareness that they’ve been manipulated.
