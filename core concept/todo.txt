1. List of Puzzles and Incorporate Into the Story
    Once the storyboarding is done, align puzzles with the flavor and setting of each region. This will add depth to exploration and reward players who engage with the world.

2. Define Ethics and NPC Interactions
    Decide how the player’s ethical choices impact gameplay. For example, decide if making certain "necessary" sacrifices influences the story or if ethical actions affect NPC relationships and reputation.

3. Ethics Twist Decision
    This is a core part of the game's theme, so explore options for a central twist that aligns with the atmosphere of adversarial mystery. For example, you could reveal that a necessary quest task was morally ambiguous, affecting the outcome.

4. Generate the Menu
    Create a draft of the user interface and navigation. A clear, well-organized menu can elevate the player experience, especially for a text-based game.

5. Define Hint Mechanics
    Decide on how hints are earned (perhaps via progress, certain achievements, or bartering). Plan a "vague-but-helpful" system that fits the game's mysterious tone.

6. List of NPCs
    Generic NPCs: Scavenger, wanderer, shopkeeper, etc., who appear in multiple zones.
    Regionally Specific NPCs: Unique to certain areas
        subtype: mythological figures
    Story-Critical NPCs: Defined characters that affect the narrative directly, such as potential allies and key quest-givers.

7. Define NPC Allies and Meeting Points (partly done in storyboard)
    Sketch out the initial meeting points and conditions for each potential ally. These allies will shape the player’s journey and affect gameplay in a team dynamic.

8. Determine Number and Nature of Endings
    Finalize the possible endings and any conditions leading to each, such as planeshift, the Dyson Sphere turning off the sun, or an endless loop. For each, define how player actions and choices throughout the game lead toward specific endings.

9. Graffiti List
    List snippets of graffiti that provide backstory and environmental context without overt exposition, reinforcing the adversarial feel.
